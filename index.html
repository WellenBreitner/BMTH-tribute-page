BRING ME
THE HORIZON

Bring Me the Horizon is a British rock band formed in Sheffield, England in 2004. The band's original lineup consisted of vocalist Oliver Sykes, guitarist Lee Malia, guitarist Curtis Ward, bassist Matt Kean, and drummer Matt Nicholls. They gained popularity in the mid-2000s with their unique blend of metalcore and deathcore. The band released their debut album, "Count Your Blessings," in 2006. It showcased their aggressive and heavy sound, characterized by Sykes' harsh vocals and the band's intense instrumentation. The album received mixed reviews but helped them establish a dedicated fanbase.


Over the years, Bring Me the Horizon underwent significant stylistic changes, incorporating elements of electronic music, alternative rock, and pop into their sound. Their second album, "Suicide Season" (2008), marked a shift towards a more melodic and accessible approach while still maintaining their heavy roots.


In 2010, the band released their critically acclaimed third album, "There Is a Hell, Believe Me I've Seen It. There Is a Heaven, Let's Keep It a Secret." This album showcased their evolving sound and featured collaborations with guest vocalists, including Lights and Josh Franceschi of You Me at Six.


Bring Me the Horizon's fourth album, "Sempiternal," released in 2013, marked a major turning point for the band. It showcased a more refined sound, incorporating electronic and atmospheric elements. The album received widespread acclaim and expanded their fanbase globally, propelling them into the mainstream.


Their fifth studio album, "That's the Spirit" (2015), further solidified their transition into alternative rock. It featured a more accessible and melodic sound, with themes of personal struggles, mental health, and self-discovery. The album was a commercial success, reaching high chart positions worldwide.


In 2019, Bring Me the Horizon released their sixth studio album, "Amo," which showcased an even broader range of musical influences, including pop, electronica, and even elements of hip-hop. The album received mixed reviews but achieved commercial success, reaching the top of the charts in several countries.


Continuing their musical exploration, Bring Me the Horizon released their seventh studio album, "Post Human: Survival Horror," in 2020. The album incorporated heavy elements from their earlier work while exploring new genres such as nu-metal and pop punk. It received positive reviews and was praised for its experimentation.


Throughout their career, Bring Me the Horizon has been recognized for their energetic live performances and innovative approach to music. They have won several awards, including two Kerrang! Awards for Best British Band.
